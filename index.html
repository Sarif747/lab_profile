About the Research

Heart failure (HF) is a chronic and progressive cardiovascular condition that places a major burden on patients, caregivers, and healthcare systems. It is one of the leading causes of hospitalization and rehospitalization, particularly among older adults, and often requires patients to manage complex treatment plans over long periods of time. Effective self-management in HF includes understanding medications, monitoring symptoms, following diet and fluid restrictions, and recognizing warning signs early enough to seek care.

Despite the importance of education in heart failure care, many patients continue to face challenges related to low health literacy, difficulty understanding medical terminology, and limited support between clinical visits. These gaps can lead to confusion, poor adherence, and preventable readmissions. Recent advances in large language models (LLMs) create new opportunities to provide more accessible, conversational, and personalized education. However, for these systems to be safe and effective in healthcare, they must be designed not only for technical accuracy, but also for trust, clarity, usability, and alignment with real patient needs.

This research explores how human-centered design principles can be integrated with LLM-based systems to improve heart failure patient education and support better self-management outcomes.

Research Objective

The main objective of this project is to investigate how AI-assisted educational tools can be designed to better support heart failure patients in understanding and managing their condition. Rather than focusing only on model performance, this work emphasizes the importance of making AI-generated responses understandable, trustworthy, and responsive to differences in patient literacy, communication preferences, and self-care challenges.

The long-term goal is to contribute to the development of a human-centered AI framework that can provide continuous education and self-management support beyond the clinical setting. By doing so, the project aims to reduce misunderstanding, strengthen adherence, and ultimately help lower avoidable readmissions in heart failure care.

Methods

  • Stakeholders: Heart failure patients, caregivers, and clinicians
  • Study Design: Exploratory study using qualitative methods to understand patient education challenges
  • Data Collection: Semi-structured interviews with patients, caregivers, and clinicians
  • Additional Sources: Data extraction from text/documents, books, articles, websites, and databases
  • Data Analysis: Thematic analysis to identify key insights from interviews, along with cleaning and processing extracted data
  • Application Development: Integration of LLMs to improve patient education, accessibility, and continuous self-management support

Literature Review Key Findings

We conducted a systematic literature review across 5 databases and extracted 53 research papers to identify challenges and limitations in heart failure patient education that will be addressed in this research.
  • Current patient education approaches are often insufficient for long-term engagement and adherence
  • Human-centered AI systems can improve accessibility, personalization, and continuity of education
  • LLM-based tools show promise, but their effectiveness depends on usability, trust, and patient-centered design
  • Existing systems often overlook real patient needs, health literacy differences, and self-management challenges
